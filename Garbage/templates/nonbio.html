Nonbio Waste

Definition

Non-bio waste refers to materials that are not biodegradable and do not decompose naturally. This type of waste typically includes synthetic and inorganic materials.

Composition

  • Plastics: Bags, bottles, containers, and packaging materials.
  • Metals: Cans, aluminum foil, and metal scraps.
  • Electronic Waste: Broken or obsolete electronics, such as computers, phones, and appliances.

Disposal Methods

  • Recycling: Many non-bio waste materials can be processed and repurposed, reducing the need for new raw materials.
  • Landfilling: Non-bio waste often ends up in landfills, where it can persist for hundreds of years, potentially causing environmental harm.
